Why TourneyBit Exists

Hi I'm Mike. I am the guy behind TourneyBit. I started TourneyBit because I love friendly competition. Competitions bring people together and bring out the best in people. I have always enjoyed participating in impromptu tournaments with my friends and family, whether it is playing ping pong or playing Mario Kart. It truly brings me joy to get involved in the minutiae of setting up the bracket, keeping score, and tracking stats.

What Is TourneyBit?

TourneyBit is how you truly own your tournament experience. You own your data, you own how you integrate, and you own where it runs. It is the only bring-your-own-cloud (BYOC) tournament management product on the market that you can host yourself, so you can be in complete control, and wow your partners and participants with a unique and unforgettable tournament experience.

At its core, TourneyBit provides a tournament and competition management platform. You can incorporate your brand and tune settings to your way of doing things. To run your tournaments, you can use the built-in web-based UI with a mobile-first and accessible design, or integrate with the API where you want to completely customize the experience. You can even embed the platform on your own website to quickly deliver a tournament experience in line with your brand.

For larger organizations, TourneyBit can help satisfy your legal requirements and your IT teams. You can deploy the software to your own infrastructure, and secure and protect your data within your own corporate network. The personal information of your participants is safely protected by your own standards and your own teams. With TourneyBit, you can limit your risk of non-compliance and security issues stemming from the use of third-party sub-processors.

For small organizations and large organizations alike, you can actually own the software you use to manage your tournaments and competitions. You pay for it once and will continue to get updates for that version. No subscription required! And the simplicity of deploying the platform limits the tradeoffs of hosting your own software — there's only a single binary to deploy, and it runs automatically (and securely) without any configuration.
